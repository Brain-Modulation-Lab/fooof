Motivations
===========

In this section, we explore the ideas and findings that motivate parameterizing neural power spectra. Using code examples, we explore some of the concepts underlying how we approach analyzing neural data and with digital signal processing; cover measurement issues whereby many current approaches may conflate different aspects of the data; and overall try to motivate why we consider parameterizing neural power spectra, or similar approaches, to be important, both conceptually and in practice.

.. _sphx_glr_auto_motivations_concepts:

Concepts
--------

Conceptually, we consider neural time series, and thus neural power spectra, to contain multiple 'components', specifically, periodic and aperiodic components, that can each vary independently. 

In these examples, we motivate that even though time series *can* be perfectly represented by a sum of sine waves, this does not imply that that power at a particular frequency in a power spectrum necessarily reflects rhythmic activity, and why it is important and interesting to also investigate aperiodic activity.

.. _sphx_glr_auto_motivations_measurements:

Measurements
------------

Using the conceptual framework whereby we consider neural data to comprise by periodic and activity activity, here we examine how this conception relates to many common analysis approaches employed with neural data.

Broadly, these examples compare an existing approach to parameterizing neural power spectra, and seek to surface if and how different methods may have trouble adjudicating between different features of the data changing.
